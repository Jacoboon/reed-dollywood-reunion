When Are We Going?

We are aiming for late spring during Dollywood's Flower & Food Festival (typically late April through early June).

Target window:

  • Last weekend of April
  • First one or two weekends of May

We will avoid holiday weekends like Memorial Day when prices and crowds spike. Dates are flexible and will be finalized after we see everyone's RSVPs.

How Long Are We Staying?

The current plan is simple:

  • 3 nights total
  • 2 days at Dollywood

Proposed structure

  • Night 1: Arrivals, easy dinner, catching up, old stories, and photos.
  • Day 1 - Dollywood: Rides, shows, Flower & Food Festival, exploring together.
  • Day 2 - Dollywood or Free Day: Finish favorites, or mix Dollywood with Pigeon Forge activities or downtime.
  • Morning 4: Big breakfast together, last photos, hugs, and travel home.

Where Will We Stay?

Three options to keep us together, comfortable, and within budget.

A. Shared Cabin (Preferred)

Large cabin near Pigeon Forge for a big chunk of the family.

  • 3-4 bedroom cabins sleeping about 8-12 people
  • Roughly $200-$400 per night before tax/fees
  • Splitting across a full cabin keeps per-person lodging low

Benefits: one hub to hang out, kitchen for shared meals, cozy living room/porch, maybe a hot tub or game room. The organizer plans to cover the cabin cost if possible.

B. Hotels

If we split into nearby hotel rooms instead:

  • Mid-range rooms often run around $100-$150 per night in spring weekends
  • Each household books its own room near the group
  • Private space and easy booking, but less "all together" time

C. Dollywood Resorts

Dollywood's DreamMore Resort & Spa and similar options:

  • Resort perks like shuttles and parking benefits
  • More themed and higher-end than standard hotels
  • Great if a smaller group wants a destination feel; cabins are usually more cost-effective for a large group

Park Tickets & Costs

Approximate numbers for planning. Final prices may shift slightly when we book.

Tickets (per person)

  • Adults: about $100 for 1 day, about $135 for 2 days
  • Kids: about $90 for 1 day, about $120 for 2 days
  • Seniors: about $95 for 1 day, about $125 for 2 days
  • Expect kids and seniors to be a bit cheaper than adult prices

Group Discounts

  • Groups of roughly 15+ on the same day may get lower rates
  • Group packages can include perks like a free ticket after a certain count
  • We will contact Dollywood group sales once RSVPs are in

Parking

  • Standard parking is around $25 per car per day
  • Example: 3 cars for 2 days is roughly $150 total
  • Carpooling helps keep the cost low per person

Food in the Park

  • Plan on about $40-$50 per person per park day
  • Covers 2 meals plus snacks (cinnamon bread, funnel cakes, etc.)
  • Eat a big breakfast first and share dishes to bring this number down

One Special Splurge: Dolly Parton's Stampede

Optional dinner show with live horses, music, and a four-course meal.

  • Typical ticket range: around $70+ per adult and $35+ per child (before tax)
  • Organizer may treat a few key family members (elders or VIPs)
  • Others are welcome to buy their own tickets if they want to join

How the Budget Works

Two budget levels guide what is covered vs. what each household handles.

Option A: Shared Costs, Everyone Chips In (Most Likely)

  • Organizer aims to pay for the shared cabin for 3 nights
  • Organizer covers their own tickets, food, and some shared groceries
  • Organizer may treat a few family members to Stampede
  • Everyone else covers travel, park tickets, park food, and can chip in for shared groceries

Comfortable target budget: around $2,000.

Option B: Maximum Treat, Organizer Covers Almost Everything

  • Organizer covers cabin, most or all park tickets, a large chunk of food, parking, and a handful of Stampede tickets
  • Everyone else mainly covers travel and personal extras (souvenirs, other attractions)

Stretch budget: up to about $4,000, decided after seeing RSVPs.

Sample Weekend Plan

Draft flow for the reunion weekend. Times are flexible once we know who's coming.

Night 1 - Arrival & Memory Night

  • Arrive at the cabin or hotel
  • Simple shared dinner (chili, pasta, or pizza)
  • Spread out photo albums and family pictures
  • Story circle: share a favorite memory or story

Day 1 - Dollywood: Flowers, Food, Fun

  • Group photo near the main entrance or a scenic spot
  • Flower & Food Festival displays, rides, shows, shops
  • Lunch together at a pre-chosen spot
  • Afternoon mini-groups by interest; evening show or sunset walk

Day 2 - Second Park Day or Mix & Match

  • Option 1: Second full Dollywood day to finish favorites
  • Option 2: Half park, half Pigeon Forge attractions
  • Evening: Stampede for those attending; games/relaxing for others

Morning 4 - Big Breakfast & Goodbyes

  • Group breakfast (pancakes, eggs, biscuits, etc.)
  • Final family photos on the porch or at a scenic overlook
  • Hugs and goodbyes as everyone heads home

The Invitation (Why This Matters)

This started as a way to turn "remember when we were all together?" into "remember when we met up at Dollywood and did it again?" We used to gather often, but life got busy and most connections are quick calls or texts.

The reunion is a chance to honor old memories, bring the younger generation into the mix, and make new stories in a special place. Tone is warm, low-pressure, and open to friends, partners, and bonus family. If you love someone in the Reed circle, you're welcome.
